```python
    for i in range(1, len(input)):
        for j in range(1, len(input[0])):
            if input[i - 1] == input[j - 1]:
                dp[i][j] = dp[i - 1][j - 1] + 1
            else:
                dp[i][j] = max(dp[i - 1][j], dp[i][j - 1])
    
    # The length of the longest common subsequence is stored in dp[-1][-1]
    return dp[-1][-1]
```

Otherwise, the length of the LCS between these two substrings is either the maximum of the lengths of the LCS between the current substring and the previous substring, or the length of the LCS between the previous substring and the substring before that.

After building up the `dp` table, the length of the LCS can be found by looking at the last cell of the table (`dp[-1][-1]`). This approach has a time complexity of O(n*m), where n and m are the lengths of the input strings.
