## Answer (1)

You can use `apply()` to apply a function on each row and then `group_by()` to group them by their first column. Then you can use `summarise()` to summarize the second column.

Here is an example:

```
library(dplyr)

df <- data.frame(x = c(rep(c(0, 9), 3), rep(c(9, 0), 2)), y = c(rep(c(0, 6), 3), rep(c(6, 0), 2)))

df %>%
  group_by(x) %>%
  summarise(y = sum(y))
# A tibble: 2 x 2
   x     y

1  0     6
2  9     6
