Comment: I'm not sure what you mean by "the network is trained to recognize the pattern". The network doesn't know anything about patterns. It just learns how to map inputs to outputs based on training data. If your training data has a lot of noise in it (i.e., randomness), then the network will learn that there are no useful patterns and will produce random results when given new inputs.

Answer: You have two problems here:
\begin{itemize}
\item Your network is too small for the problem at hand.
\item Your learning rate is way too high.
\end{itemize}
